Our Philosophy

New paths for the benefit of the patient!


New paths for the benefit of the patient! In the last few decades, the field of tumor immunology has been intensively researched all over the world. The Nobel Prize in Medicine was awarded in 2011 and 2018 for immuno-oncological research, demonstrating the outstanding importance of this discipline.
Ralph Steinman received the Nobel Prize for the discovery of dendritic cells and many other awards and recognition. (https://ru.qwe.wiki/wiki/Ralph_M._Steinman ) American James Alison and Japanese Tasuku Honze were awarded for the revolutionary method of cancer immunotherapy using T cells. (https://www.bbc.com/russian/news-45706862)
(First approval of antibody therapy for cancer: trastuzumab / herceptin® for metastatic breast cancer and Her2 / neu overexpression (USA, 1998) First approval of vaccination to prevent cervical cancer with vaccines against HPV Gardasil® (USA, 6/2006) and Cervarix® (Australia, 5/2007); (Medizinnobelpreis 2008 for Harald zur Hausen for the discovery of the cervical cancer launch uterus with human papillomaviruses)

Experience shows that we are on a right and successfully developing path: with the help of the method of immunotherapy with dendritic cells, we can offer our patients an individual therapy that offers promising prospects in their fight against cancer - as sparing treatment as possible without side effects, as well as prolongation of life in a qualitative sense.

From the very beginning, we were impressed by the complex qualities of immunotherapy. It utilizes and strengthens the patient's own defense system against cancer and steadily activates the immune system against newly growing cancer cells.

In contrast, surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy are all aimed at destroying the tumor with surgery or medication - often with serious side effects on healthy cells and the immune system.
As an individualized treatment, dendritic cell immunotherapy is primarily intended for people who want to actively participate in their own healing.
People who deliberately choose and identify with therapy also indirectly influence the healing process and thus have a positive impact on recovery.

It is this vision of a natural and sustainable protective cancer treatment that inspires us every day over and over again brings a desire of our team to help the patient discover new, extraordinary, innovative solutions to fight cancer. After all, this disease disrupts functioning of the whole body. Therefore, along with the method of treatment with dendritic cells, we also offer our dear patients supportive, but no less important and effective therapies such as physiotherapy, Ayuverda, yoga, various types of meditation, hypnotherapy, nutritional and occupational therapies.

Immunotherapy with dendritic cells

The immune system helps people stay healthy by recognizing pathogens such as bacteria and viruses, as well as cancer cells, as harmful and killing them. It has been known for some time that the so-called antigen-presenting cells are necessary for the recognition of foreign bodies (antigens). These antigen-presenting cells inform the immune system of the invasion and activate other immune cells to fight it in a variety of ways.

Treatment process

Before starting therapy, an updated serology of infection for HIV, hepatitis B and C must be performed. Even if the infection status is known, we are obliged to retest it shortly before starting therapy in a certified laboratory, which has been reported to our regulatory authorities for reasons of product safety and occupational safety ...
After obtaining monocytes, they are cultured in the laboratory according to various protocols and differentiated into dendritic cells or tolerogenic or regenerating macrophages. Depending on the protocol, patients receive their "trained" cells back in 4-7 days.
Treatment is usually given as a baseline immunization three times, one day apart, for one week.

Rehabilitation after cancer

Cancer rehabilitation is aimed at Patients who have completed primary cancer treatment and who wish to improve their physical and mental health. Each victim responds differently to the cancer problem and its treatment. Thus, in a holistic and interdisciplinary approach, treatment goals are jointly defined in order to gain new strength to return to professional and personal daily life.
