Rehabilitation after cancer

Cancer rehabilitation is aimed at Patients who have completed primary cancer treatment and who wish to improve their physical and mental health. Each victim responds differently to the cancer problem and its treatment. Thus, in a holistic and interdisciplinary approach, treatment goals are jointly defined in order to gain new strength to return to professional and personal daily life. Cancer rehabilitation offers a wide range of therapeutic and diagnostic measures. The focus is on therapeutic exercise therapy / sports, sensorimotor skills (especially for patients with severe symptoms of polyneuropathy) and psychosocial support.


Physiotherapy

The exercise program for the patient is carried out by physiotherapists with various additional qualifications:
Remedial gymnastics: under guidance, weakened muscles are strengthened and mobility is improved. The patterns of physiological movements are again worked out, training of the cardiovascular system, as well as the coordinating and mobilization aspects, are included. This whole body workout uses a variety of aids such as balls, sticks or tera bands.

Strength Training: Different machines use different exercises to build muscle. This means that physical complaints can also be avoided as a preventive measure. Besides building muscle, strength and endurance are also increased.

Endurance training: Bicycle ergometer exercises and Nordic walking allow you to consistently train your endurance. Coordination training: Various coordination skills (e.g. balance, orientation, rhythm, responsiveness) are trained with or without assisting devices (balls, MFT disc, BalancePad, etc.)


Ergotherapy

During cancer rehabilitation, occupational therapy focuses on the patient's daily activities. In order to do this satisfactorily in daily life, different abilities and skills are required. It is on these abilities and skills that occupational therapy is focused. Exercise and activity purposefully promote well-being and health.

Individual therapy: With a help of special methods (for example, fine motor training, sensitivity training, cognitive training, ergonomic recommendations for the workplace), individual goals that are relevant to everyday life are achieved.

Therapy Seminar: With a targeted proposition in which “being active” and “having fun” are at the forefront, new positive experiences can be gained at all levels.


Clinical psychology and health psychology

Counseling / Treatment: Psychological conversations have a calming effect and help you find a personal way to deal with cancer and your current life situation. In one-on-one psychological conversation, time and space is provided for those the patient is currently dealing with or would like to deal with. These topics of conversation may include planning health behavior, strengthening individual resources, coping with illness, personal, family, or professional crises.

Psychological training: During the training, psychological knowledge about health is transmitted, which helps to cope with cancer and associated stressors. Diet recommendations

During the course of cancer and its treatment, changes in nutritional requirements can be observed. In oncological rehabilitation, specific therapeutic nutritional measures are discussed and agreed upon one-on-one with a nutritionist.


Therapeutic massage / special massage

Therapeutic massage completes the rehabilitation program by relaxing muscles and affecting circulation, but also stimulating mobility and agility. If indicated, you can also use special types of massage, such as manual lymphatic drainage, massage of the reflex zone (feet) or massage of the connective tissue.


Yoga

Studies have been conducted by the US State Cancer Institute (NCI) on the effects of yoga on women with breast cancer. Sixty patients diagnosed with breast cancer took part in the first stage of the research. Half of them did yoga regularly for two months, the other half of the women did not receive any additional stress. The research results showed that women who practiced yoga improved their health, increased activity, increased energy and a complete absence of depressive moods. The second stage of the experiment was repeated with the participation of one hundred and twenty women, half of them did yoga, and the second did not. The results were confirmed, except that these studies showed that women in the first group who practiced yoga were physically stronger and better tolerated the side effects of treatment. Yoga for breast cancer helps patients fight the consequences of cancer treatment (chemotherapy, radiation, postoperative problems). - Increases tone and relieves drowsiness, gives strength to further fight the disease. - Breathing practices help to cope with nausea and dizziness, give calmness and confidence. If you practice yoga even for a short time, it still gives a tangible effect. Classes combine meditation, postures (asanas) and breathing practices (pranayamas). Meditation is extremely important for people with cancer. This disease is hard on the body and, in fact, is a stress that hits a woman's immunity. By meditating for at least twenty minutes a day, you can achieve that stress will not remain and the immune system will be protected. Immunotherapy with dendritic cells

The immune system helps people stay healthy by recognizing pathogens such as bacteria and viruses, as well as cancer cells, as harmful and killing them. It has been known for some time that the so-called antigen-presenting cells are necessary for the recognition of foreign bodies (antigens). These antigen-presenting cells inform the immune system of the invasion and activate other immune cells to fight it in a variety of ways.
